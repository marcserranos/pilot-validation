## Open questions

### Strategic
- **AoU-native callset: trust & sequencing.** AoU already ships a full-cohort srWGS HLA callset (HLA-HD + Polysolver + OptiType) — this reframes the "massive calling is our contribution" pitch. Fork: **(a)** if AoU-native holds up vs. long-read → pivot toward validate-then-build-downstream on the existing calls (faster path to the aims); **(b)** if it shows ancestry-correlated discordance → the calling work becomes *more* necessary and the bias finding is itself novel (every existing AoU-derived HLA study would inherit that bias). **Lean: validate against long-read first** — an ancestry-distribution study on AoU's calls is circular if we don't yet know whether those calls are ancestry-biased in exactly the dimension being studied. Questions for supervisors: were they aware AoU ships this? any known ancestry benchmarks of that specific ensemble? validate-first vs. study-first preference? which outcome do they expect (shapes whether the pilot needs to be stratified/larger to detect ancestry bias vs. just overall concordance)? (raised 2026-07-09).
  - **n=3 evidence (2026-07-09, EXPERIMENTS.md):** AoU-native is broadly concordant for Class I / DQB1 / DPA1, **but is the lone outlier at DQA1 in 3/3 people** — and this one is cross-technology robust: a short-read caller (SpecHLA) *and* a long-read caller (SpecImmune) agree against it, so it isn't a short-read-shared-bias artifact. Exactly the locus-specific bias the "validate first" stance guards against — concrete early support for validating before building downstream on AoU's calls, at least locus-by-locus. Not yet ancestry-resolved.
  - **Mechanism decided 2026-07-09: Experiment D (EXPERIMENTS.md roadmap) is now how this gets answered at scale** — the ancestry-stratified 3-way comparison directly targets whether the DQA1 bias (and the DPA1 divergence) is ancestry-correlated or universal. **The supervisor conversation is no longer a blocking prerequisite for starting that pilot** (see Resolved below) — it stays open and valuable, just not gating.
  - **Ancestry-distribution evidence (2026-07-10, EXPERIMENTS.md Experiment A extension):** the two loci split cleanly. DPA1's homozygosity varies ~2.5x by ancestry (afr 27% → eur 68%), tracking the expected Out-of-Africa diversity gradient — looks like real population structure, not an AoU-native calling artifact. DQA1 shows no such gradient (flat ~18-19% across afr/amr/eas/eur/sas) — consistent with its discordance being method/database-specific rather than population-genetic. Doesn't resolve whether the actual SpecHLA/SpecImmune-vs-AoU-native divergences are ancestry-correlated — still Experiment D's job.
  - **Experiment D result, n=60 (2026-07-12, EXPERIMENTS.md): DQA1 question answered, and reassuringly.** The ancestry gradient is real and large (AoU-native's lone-outlier rate: 20% AFR/SAS → 90% EAS, 70% EUR) — but the resolution-tier analysis (`analyze_experiment_d_resolution.py`, SpecImmune-LR as truth) shows every one of AoU-native's "wrong" DQA1 calls in this cohort is still in the right allele family (58% close, 0% partial/full-miss) — it's bounded imprecision, not an unreliable call. This softens the "validate before build" urgency specifically at DQA1 (the errors are safe, not silent-wrong), though the ancestry-correlation itself is still real and worth knowing about. **DRB1 turned out to be the more important finding, unexpectedly:** AoU-native's DRB1 misses are also mostly near-misses (34% close, 0% full-miss), but SpecHLA's are not (49% partial-miss, 6% full-miss, real wrong-allele-family errors) — meaning DRB1's overall poor 3-way agreement is being driven by SpecHLA specifically, not shared difficulty. This is new, gene-specific evidence for the "validate-then-build" fork: AoU-native looks safer than SpecHLA at DRB1, safer than initially assumed at DQA1, and the right framing is per-locus trust, not a single verdict on AoU-native as a whole.
  - **Callset validation report, 2026-07-19 (EXPERIMENTS.md, `reports/aou_callset_validation/README.md`): independent, population-genetics-based corroboration that DRB1 specifically is the weak locus.** Separate from Experiment D's cross-tool comparison — this checked the AoU-native callset itself against properties real HLA data must satisfy (allelic diversity, 3 external published-frequency anchors, Hardy-Weinberg proxy, IMGT catalogue match rate). Every check passed except one: DRB1 (and, to a lesser extent, B) show excess homozygosity that survives ancestry stratification (not just a pooling artifact), while DPA1's apparent excess is confirmed as real population structure (obs/exp ~1.0 in every ancestry group). Two methods that share no assumptions — cross-tool comparison and population genetics — now agree DRB1 is the locus to trust least. Strengthens the existing "per-locus trust, not a single verdict" lean; does not change it. Also found: AoU's own GIAB-based QC report does not benchmark HLA specifically, so this pilot remains the only HLA-locus reliability evidence for the dataset. The strategic fork itself (build downstream directly on AoU-native vs. call independently) is still an open call, now better-informed.
- **Can SpecImmune-LR serve as the validation reference on this data?** The pilot treats long-read as "presumed most-accurate," and that prior is reasonable for this region. At n=3 SpecImmune-LR diverges from the short-read consensus at 6/24 loci — but **concordance cannot tell us whether those are SpecImmune errors or short-read blind spots**, because the two agreeing methods (AoU + SpecHLA) share short-read data and so aren't independent corroboration. Divergences range from deeper resolution (expected, trust it) to a different allele *group* (A\*02 vs A\*24, warrants scrutiny). **To adjudicate: pull per-call confidence (`One_guess` vs ambiguous) + measure per-locus long-read depth from the sliced BAMs.** Only then can we say whether SpecImmune is a clean reference here, or whether its reliability is coverage/config-limited (8× HiFi on `grch38_noalt`, missing the ALT contigs its docs want). Do **not** dismiss SpecImmune divergences as "over-resolution" — resolving deeper is the point of using long reads. (raised 2026-07-09).
- **Data egress / compliance.** HLA genotypes are near-fingerprint-identifying; even small derived files may need AoU egress/publication review before leaving the Workbench, regardless of size. Not confirmed with the Controlled-Tier sponsor. Until then: do comparison/analysis *inside* the Workbench, don't download results.
- **Bare `person_id`s in a public repo.** Operational commands (STATUS, EXPERIMENTS) carry bare research_ids. Genotypes are quarantined to the gitignored `.local.md`, but whether bare ids belong in a *public* repo at all is unresolved — flag to the sponsor alongside egress. (Current practice preserved, not expanded; the ids are already in git history.)

### Methodological
- **minimap2 vs bwa for SpecImmune read-binning.** `--align_method_1 minimap2` = faster (gap shrinks as padding narrows: ~33min at fullpad → ~1.7min at pad5k) but caused a real DPB1 regression on person 1017156. **2026-07-09 update (person 2522883, full aligner x padding sweep, EXPERIMENTS.md):** DPB1 did NOT regress — both aligners gave identical calls/identity at every padding level, and no gene showed any accuracy difference between aligners in this person. At n=2, leans toward "one-off, not systematic," but **still not adopted as default — confirm on a 3rd person** before changing the standing choice.
  - **2026-07-10: Experiment C run in both aligner variants, sequentially** (`run_experiment_c_comparison.sh` = bwa, then `run_experiment_c_comparison_minimap2.sh` = minimap2 once the bwa run fully completes), reusing the existing pad100k_bwa/pad100k_minimap2 baselines. Deliberately not run concurrently — this is a timing experiment, and contending for the VM's 4 vCPUs would contaminate the exact measurement being taken. The minimap2 leg is an extra, opportunistic data point riding along with the restricted-DB test — **not itself the n=3 confirmation this open question still needs**, and its result should be read as directional. **Long-run objective this is feeding toward (not yet decided): if minimap2 keeps matching bwa's accuracy while staying substantially faster, fully deprecate bwa for SpecImmune-LR** rather than keep maintaining two supported aligners.
- **Region padding — real data now available (2026-07-09, EXPERIMENTS.md).** pad100k (3.9x smaller than current 4Mb default) showed zero degradation across all 8 genes in person 2522883, and real runtime savings (~21min bwa vs ~50min). pad40k and pad5k showed measurable degradation on 2 of 8 genes (Gene A ambiguity explosion, DRB1 identity loss) — a real, not hypothetical, floor. **Candidate to adopt pad100k as new default padding for SpecImmune-LR — pending confirmation on ≥1 more person** (this is n=1 for the padding dimension).
- **SpecHLA-SR padding — separate finding, do not conflate with the LR result above (2026-07-10, EXPERIMENTS.md Experiment B).** Real fragment-size floor measured at 650bp (p99), ~50x smaller than the LR floor — as expected, since short reads are ~50x shorter than long reads. Zero call degradation observed at ANY of the 8 genes down to 325bp padding (below the measured floor), unlike the LR sweep which found real degradation at DRB1/Gene A. **Candidate to adopt pad2000-pad10000 as new default padding for SpecHLA-SR specifically — pending confirmation on ≥1 more person.** SpecHLA and SpecImmune now have two independently-measured, tool-specific padding recommendations — they should not be unified into one "padding" default.
  - **Open hypothesis, not tested (Marc, 2026-07-12, EXPERIMENTS.md Experiment D entry):** Experiment D ran all 60 people at pad10000 and found SpecHLA specifically breaks down at DRB1 (49% partial-miss + 6% full-miss vs SpecImmune truth — see the "AoU-native callset" entry above). This *could* be the "≥1 more person" confirmation this padding candidate was waiting on turning up a real, locus-specific failure — or it could be an artifact of pad10000 itself being too narrow for DRB1's paralog-crowded region (DRB3/4/5), by the same read-misassignment mechanism already confirmed for SpecImmune's gene-panel restriction (Experiment C: removing decoy pseudogene references caused reproducible miscalls at exactly Gene A and DRB1). The original Experiment B sweep (n=1, exact-2-field-match only) could not have caught this even if it were true — it never measured DRB1-specific degradation with a resolution-tier lens, and reused a single person. **Not adopted or rejected — needs a direct test:** re-run SpecHLA at a wider padding level for people already run at pad10000, resolution-tier-compare against SpecImmune truth, see if DRB1's partial/full-miss rate improves with more padding. Until then, treat pad10000 as unconfirmed specifically at DRB1, even though it's otherwise the leading candidate default.
- **4-field vs 2-field resolution** — is 4-field actually required downstream, or is 2-field enough for the frequency/PRS aims? **Partially resolved 2026-07-10 (EXPERIMENTS.md, Experiment A):** AoU-native is structurally capped at 3-field (99.4-100% of calls per locus, zero 4-field calls across 535,658 people) — if 4-field turns out to be required, AoU-native cannot supply it under any circumstances and SpecHLA/SpecImmune become load-bearing, not just a validation check. Still open: whether the aims actually need 4-field.
  - **Resolution vs. generalization in the disease-sanity-check model itself (Marc, 2026-07-21, not yet started).** A more specific version of this question, raised while discussing `hla_disease_sanity_check.py`'s design (`reports/disease_sanity_check/`): that model's L1 logistic regression treats every allele as a flat, independent one-hot dummy — two alleles sharing a family (same first field, e.g. `B*39:10` vs `B*39:13`, both present independently in the narcolepsy top-feature list) get zero shared structure, and `to2field()` currently discards 3rd-field resolution entirely even though AoU-native's raw calls carry it for most loci (per the "Partially resolved" note above). Open research question: would keeping 3rd-field resolution actually improve out-of-fold generalization on people the model hasn't seen, or is the extra granularity mostly noise (rarer, sparser columns, same L1 regularization)? Proposed design, not yet built: paired 5-fold CV (same folds/seed) across three arms — current 2-field flat one-hot (baseline), 3-field flat one-hot, and a hierarchical/grouped-penalty version (family-level term + shrunk per-subtype deviation, e.g. group lasso) — comparing pooled and per-ancestry OOF AUROC/AUPRC. **Must sweep `C` separately per arm rather than reusing the current fixed `C=0.1`** — 3-field's larger, sparser feature space is unlikely to be equally well-regularized by the same fixed value, and reusing it would confound "does resolution help" with "was regularization mismatched to feature count." Also worth watching specifically per-ancestry: rarer alleles skew ancestry-specific, so finer resolution could plausibly widen the existing ancestry gap (Celiac's EUR-vs-AFR AUROC gap, first entry above) rather than close it. Not scoped or scheduled — a research idea to pick up deliberately, not a queued task.
- **Reference panel choice** and its effect on miscalls in non-European ancestries.
- **`grch38_noalt` compatibility.** lrWGS BAMs are aligned to `grch38_noalt` (no ALT/HLA contigs); SpecImmune's docs want ALT contigs for divergent-allele sensitivity. No workaround (AoU ships no ALT-contig lrWGS). Watch long-read call completeness/confidence vs. short-read in the bake-off.
- **Ancestry variable for the scale-up.** Prefer AoU's genetic-ancestry TSV (PCA-based AFR/AMR/EAS/EUR/MID/SAS/OTH — 7 categories, keyed by research_id) over the self-reported race field the original cohort query used, for the stratified n=25/100 pick.
- **Compute backend for scaling** — Workbench-native likely; GPU is a mismatch (HLA calling is CPU/IO-bound). Needs a per-method cost model: AoU-native = $0 egress (pre-computed variant tier); SpecHLA/SpecImmune both pull raw CRAM/BAM = egress cost.
  - **Cost-optimization research pass (2026-07-24, not yet acted on).** Ranked levers: (1) move at-scale runs off the single interactive Workbench VM onto Cloud Batch + Spot VMs once the pipeline is stable enough not to need step-by-step debugging (Cloud Life Sciences, the older managed-genomics-batch API, is fully shut down as of July 2025 — Batch is the only current path); (2) close out the two padding candidates already sitting one confirmation-run away in this file (pad100k SpecImmune-LR, pad2000-10000 SpecHLA-SR) before scaling, cheaper to confirm now than re-run thousands of people later; (3) preferentially route the ~86% assembly-holding cohort through Immuannot (~2x faster) over SpecImmune-LR. VM right-sizing (current 4vCPU/25GB) flagged as unverified, not yet worth acting on. A companion runbook for the actual 2/4/8-core scaling measurement exists but was never run: `scripts/run_core_scaling_experiment.sh`.
- **Genomic Quality Report (v9)** — has GiaB-based QC/coverage/sensitivity methodology relevant to the coverage/error-profile aim. Only archived v6–v8 versions found so far; look for v9 in the Workbench UI.
- **Assembly-based HLA typing as a future direction.** The ~10,039 revio people stuck behind the `/pacbio/` pattern aren't missing data — they have de novo phased diploid genome assemblies (`assembly_hap1_fa`/`assembly_hap2_fa`) and assembly-derived VCFs instead of a read-level aligned BAM. Our current tools (SpecHLA, SpecImmune) are read-based and can't use this directly. A genuinely different, assembly-based HLA-typing approach (e.g. aligning IMGT allele references against the phased contigs) could in principle unlock ~10x more long-read people — a real research idea, not a queued task; would need its own design and validation, not assumed to be worth building by default.
  - **Format-conversion analysis (2026-07-20, Marc's question: can these be converted to a BAM SpecImmune can use?).** No — not meaningfully. Checked SpecImmune's actual argparse (`reference/README_SpecImmune.md`): its real required input is FASTQ reads (`-r`), not BAM — `ExtractReads.sh -i <bam_or_cram>` is only an optional pre-step that extracts reads and converts to FASTQ. BAM/CRAM/FASTQ/uBAM all interconvert freely because they all preserve individual reads, which is what SpecImmune's read-binning + per-read-depth consensus/confidence model (`Reads_num`, `Match_info` identity) actually consumes. A phased assembly FASTA is the opposite — one already-collapsed consensus sequence per haplotype, with the underlying read evidence gone. You can `minimap2 -a` the two contigs against GRCh38 and get a file with a `.bam` extension, but it holds ~2 megabase-scale alignment records, nothing like a real reads-BAM, and SpecImmune's binning/confidence logic isn't built for that. **Conclusion: not a conversion problem — the assembly-based typing approach above (direct IMGT-vs-contig alignment) is the real path for these people, not a SpecImmune input hack.**
  - **Literature/software research (2026-07-20, subagent, independent web research — not yet independently verified by us).** A ready-to-use tool for exactly this may already exist: **Immuannot** (github.com/YingZhou001/Immuannot, Zhou & Li, Genome Research 2024) — minimap2-based, aligns IPD-IMGT/HLA reference gene sequences against phased assembly contigs and picks the best-matching star allele (or flags novel sequence). Reportedly already used by HPRC itself to annotate its own release assemblies (companion Zenodo dataset of Immuannot-called HPRC/CPC-p1 genotypes), reports 97.3% sensitivity / 98.1-98.5% precision on classical HLA genes, ~4min/haploid genome. A PacBio tool, `pb-StarPhase`, is a possible actively-maintained alternative but its assembly-contig-input support wasn't confirmed (docs describe aligned-read input; worth checking directly). No dedicated graph-genotyping tool (assembly vs. a population allele panel, more robust than best-hit alignment when the true allele isn't in IMGT) was found for this specific input shape. **Not adopted or evaluated yet — this is a lead to test, not a decision.** If Immuannot holds up on hands-on testing (gene coverage, license, integration effort vs. our pipeline's conventions), it looks like meaningfully less work than building the "align IMGT alleles against contigs" approach from scratch ourselves.
  - **Evaluated 2026-07-22 (Experiment F, EXPERIMENTS.md): confirmed real HLA typing, ~2x faster than SpecImmune-LR, but not adopted as a replacement.** 60-person pilot on the same Experiment D cohort: 57/60 completed (median 6.9 vs SpecImmune-LR's 13.9 min/person). Cross-method Field 2 concordance 65-94% for most genes, 40% at DRB1 — consistent with, not contradicting, DRB1's already-established status as the weakest locus. This is agreement, not accuracy (no ground truth here — Aleix's HLA-Resolve/Lai arm is still the actual accuracy check). **Practical read: Immuannot looks viable as a second, faster long-read cross-check for the assembly-holding ~86% of the cohort, not yet a basis to drop SpecImmune-LR** — whether to formally add it as a 4th pipeline arm vs. keep it as a QC cross-check is still open. Full writeup: `reports/immuannot_pilot/README.md`.
  - **Follow-on, 2026-07-24: used Immuannot as a second truth source (not just SpecImmune-LR) for the field-cascade re-scoring, then repeated both under a strict, mathematically-grounded confidence filter.** See the new "Confidence-matched truth comparison" Resolved entry below — the DRB1 apparent-improvement-under-Immuannot-truth finding is plausibly a selection artifact, not real; DQA1's AoU-native weakness is now cross-validated under two independent truth sources. Reports: `reports/experiment_d_field_cascade/README.md`, `reports/confidence_matched_truth/README.md`.

## Resolved decisions

- **Confidence-matched truth comparison: how to factor caller uncertainty into cross-method benchmarking (2026-07-24).** One global (not per-gene) threshold per tool, mathematically grounded in HiFi/Revio sequencing-error math and IMGT's real minimum inter-allele spacing (as low as 1nt) rather than picked by eye: SpecImmune-LR `si_identity >= 0.9995` + not tied; Immuannot `template_distance = 0` + no `template_warning`. Sample-size matched by equal absolute count, measured against the one shared denominator (480 = 60 people x 8 genes) for both tools — not each tool's own resolved pool, which would silently reward Immuannot's higher abstention rate. DRB1 is nearly wiped out by the bar under both truth sources (confirms it's genuinely the hardest locus, not a per-tool quirk); AoU's DQA1 weakness and SpecHLA's DQA1 strength both survive the filter. Script: `scripts/analyze_confidence_matched_truth.py`. Full writeup: `reports/confidence_matched_truth/README.md`. **Caveat: as of this writing the report's 2 figure PNGs were never actually copied from the VM into `reports/confidence_matched_truth/figures/` — the folder is empty and the README's figure references are currently dangling. Fix before trusting this as "done."**

- **All 14,521 long-read people have a real, existing aligned GRCh38 BAM — the previous 2,763-person floor (ENVIRONMENT.md quirk #13, 2026-07-11) is obsolete (2026-07-21).** `scripts/lr_manifest_format_census.py` (checkpointed, resumable, parallelized — see ENVIRONMENT.md quirk #22) ran an exhaustive existence check across all 29 primary data-bearing columns of the full v9 lrWGS manifest (not just the 2 previously-known BAM columns), for every one of 15,424 rows / 14,521 unique people. Result: every one of the 9 distinct per-person data profiles found includes `grch38_bam`, summing to the full 14,521 — and separately, **0 people have none of the 29 recognized file types**, so nobody is genuinely without usable long-read data. Before trusting a result this different from a carefully-established prior number, directly spot-checked the exact case quirk #13 had named as dangling — person 1008366's `revio`-labeled row, documented then as pointing at a nonexistent path — and confirmed it now resolves as real (`grch38_bam=1` in this run's checkpoint). **Conclusion: AoU backfilled the previously-missing `revio`-platform aligned BAMs sometime between 2026-07-11 and 2026-07-21** — consistent with quirk #13's own closing note that this dataset's publishing pattern changes over time as a normal consequence of active development. One case was directly spot-checked, not an exhaustive re-audit of all 14,521 — but the clean, universal pattern (every profile bucket includes the BAM, no partial/mixed group) is a strong, coherent signal, not a coincidence. **Practical implication: the long-read validation cohort is no longer capped near ~2,763 — all 14,521 people are viable for the existing SpecImmune-LR pipeline**, a >5x increase over the previous working assumption, including for the MID/SAS ancestry groups that were the practical bottleneck for Experiment D. Full writeup, per-platform data-shape table, and caveats: `reports/lr_data_census/README.md`. Companion/index files (`.bai` etc.) were not individually verified, and "file exists" was not checked for content/integrity — see the report's caveats section.

- **SpecImmune gene-panel restriction: confirmed feasible, 2026-07-08 "ruled out" call overturned (2026-07-10).** Read `make_db.py`/`main.py`'s actual source (local clone, not the VM). The `--HLA_fa`/`--HLA_exon_fa` mechanism genuinely restricts the DB, and every per-gene typing stage discovers genes dynamically from the `--db` folder's contents (`get_folder_list()`), not a hardcoded list — restriction really works. - **SpecImmune gene-panel restriction: NOT adopted — real VM comparison (both aligners, person 2522883) shows it's slower and less accurate (2026-07-10).** The mechanism works (confirmed above), but restricting to the 8 classical genes made SpecImmune slower in both bwa (+23%) and minimap2 (+18.5%) runs, and produced reproducible miscalls at Gene A and DRB1 in *both* aligners — same wrong alleles at A regardless of aligner, same allele-family shift at DRB1. Likely mechanism: A and DRB1 have the closest pseudogene/paralog relatives among the 8 classical genes (A: ~10 related class-I pseudogenes; DRB1: DRB3/4/5), and removing those decoy references from the panel redirects previously-absorbed ambiguous reads onto the wrong allele instead of eliminating wasted work.
